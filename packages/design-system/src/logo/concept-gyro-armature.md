# Concept — Gyroscopic Armature

**The idea.** A gyroscope/gimbal: an upright steadying ring and a tilted orbiting ring, both pierced by a single vertical needle — a precision instrument whose whole job is to stay upright no matter how it is turned.
**Why it reads "Metador."** The vertical needle is the keel spine that keeps the vessel level; the gimbal rings make self-righting *visible* — structure that holds the line under any weather, which is exactly the brand thesis (calm, dark, honest, a navigation instrument not a casino).
**How it survives 16px.** Pure circle+line geometry, one consistent 5-unit stroke, minimal anchors, and a needle that protrudes clearly past both rings so the vertical axis stays legible even when the ring crossings blur into a dense dot.
**Divergence — vs Hyperliquid blob.** No organic blob/butterfly mass: this is hard, rotational, engineered linework with a dominant straight axis — instrument, not amorphous shape.
**Divergence — vs Sui drop.** No teardrop, no fluid silhouette, no single closed bulb: two intersecting rings on an asymmetric tilt around a rigid needle read as metal and motion, the opposite of water at rest.
